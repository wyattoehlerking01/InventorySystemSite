The Hidden Costs of Inventory Spreadsheets

Mar 28, 2026 • 6 min read

Schools spend millions every year on supplies—textbooks, lab equipment, art materials, safety gear. Most track it all in a spreadsheet.

The problem? Spreadsheets are built for simple data, not complex inventory operations. As schools grow, the costs mount silently:

Time wasted on manual updates

Someone has to update the inventory every time an item moves. In a real school, that's dozens of transactions a day across multiple locations. One coordinator told us she spends 3-4 hours a week just updating spreadsheets. That's 150+ hours a year just on data entry.

Multiply that by your supply coordinator's salary, and you're looking at $3,000-$5,000 annually just on manual updates—per school.

Decision paralysis from bad data

By the time a spreadsheet is updated, the data is stale. Teachers order new supplies because they don't see what's already in stock nearby. Departments duplicate purchases. Admins can't answer simple questions: "How much did we spend on lab equipment this year?" or "Which classrooms need restocking?"

Result: wasted money on duplicate purchases, inefficient spending, and poor decisions based on incomplete information.

No visibility to prevent shortages

Without real-time tracking, critical supplies run out unexpectedly. Teachers show up to teach and can't because materials aren't available. Emergency orders cost more. Activities get cancelled or postponed.

The real cost? Disrupted learning, stressed teachers, and an unhappy principal.

The risk of loss and error

Spreadsheets are fragile. One accidentally deleted formula, one person away for a week, one file version conflict—and your inventory data breaks. You can't trust it anymore. You have to do a physical recount to figure out what you actually have.

What a real system costs

A real inventory system costs roughly $3,500-$8,000 per year for a school. That seems like a lot—until you add up the real costs of spreadsheets:

  • Coordinator time: 150+ hours annually = $2,500-$4,000
  • Duplicate purchases: 10-15% of supply budgets = $3,000-$8,000
  • Emergency orders & expedited shipping: $1,000-$3,000
  • Lost productivity from shortages: Difficult to quantify, but real

Total hidden cost: $6,500-$15,000+ per year per school. That's 2-4x the cost of a proper system.

The path forward

Schools don't need expensive enterprise software. They need a system purpose-built for their reality: classrooms, teachers, budgets, multiple locations. A system that makes it easy to track supplies, prevents shortages before they happen, and gives admins the visibility they need.

That's why we built TBD. Not to replace spreadsheets with complexity. But to give schools a tool that's actually designed for how they work.
